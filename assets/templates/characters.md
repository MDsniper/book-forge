# Characters (fiction)

> For nonfiction: repurpose this as case-study figures, exemplar personas, or (self-help) reader archetypes.

## Registry

For each character: role, arc (start → end, must be a real change), voice markers, relationships.

### <Character Name>
- **Role:** protagonist | antagonist | foil | ally | mentor | supporting
- **Want:** what they say they want (the surface goal).
- **Need:** what they actually need (the deeper truth they don't see).
- **Lie they believe:** the false belief they start with (the engine of the arc).
- **Ghost / wound:** the backstory hurt driving their present behavior.
- **Arc:** <start state> → <end state> (must be a real change — Stability Trap).
- **Voice markers:** <how they speak — vocabulary, rhythm, tics, what they never say>.
- **Distinctive gesture:** <a physical habit that's theirs alone>.
- **Relationships:** <to other named characters, with the dynamic>.

## Relationship map
- Who is allied with whom, who's in tension, who's in secret alliance.
- Where relationships **change** over the book (and how).

## Arcs must change (Stability Trap check)
- For each POV character: how are they **truly different** at the end? (Not just "they learned a lesson." Changed, damaged, or having chosen.)
- If a character ends the book unchanged, either cut them or give them a real change.
