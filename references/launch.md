# Launch phase (90-day marketing orchestrator)

Ported from authorclaw's `launch-orchestrator.ts`. The 90-day launch state machine, the marketing asset bundle, and the per-platform ad copy specs. Used by the `publish` phase after the manuscript + metadata are ready.

This phase produces the **marketing kit** and a **timeline of actions**. It does **not** execute anything on real platforms on your behalf — every external action is a step the human takes, with the asset ready and the disclosure/risk noted.

## The 90-day launch timeline

Phases from authorclaw's `LaunchPhase` enum: `draft_ready → cover_done → metadata_drafted → keywords_chosen → pre_order_live → arc_seeded → launch_day → follow_up_30 → follow_up_60 → follow_up_90 → complete`.

Day offsets from launch day (0):

| Day | Action | Channel | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| −(preOrder+14) | Draft KDP metadata | Internal | low |
| −(preOrder+10) | Harvest keywords from comp-title ASINs | Amazon (read-only) | low |
| −preOrder (default −30) | Set up KDP pre-order | Amazon KDP | **high** — disclosures: ai_generated_text, ai_generated_art |
| −21 | Generate ARC list & BookFunnel delivery | — | medium — disclosure: reader_data |
| −21 | Send ARC to reader team | Email ESP | **high** |
| **0** | Flip KDP pre-order to live | Amazon KDP | **critical** |
| **0** | Send launch email to main list | Email ESP | **high** |
| **0** | Post launch-day social content | Social | medium |
| +1 | Kick off AMS ad campaigns | Amazon Ads | **high** — disclosure: financial_action |
| +7 | Price pulse: launch → $4.99 | Amazon KDP | **high** |
| +14 | Follow-up email to engaged readers | Email ESP | medium |
| +30 | AMS campaign review + optimization | Amazon Ads | medium |
| +60 | Mid-tail email + social recap | Email ESP + Social | medium |
| +90 | BookBub Featured Deal submission (if eligible) | BookBub | **high** |

**Critical design rule (from authorclaw):** *"This service does NOT execute anything irreversible on its own. Every step that writes to an external platform creates a ConfirmationRequest and WAITS for user approval."* Each step has a dry-run diff + rollback note. KDP publish rollback: *"KDP publish is effectively irreversible. You can unpublish within 90 days but copies already sold remain with buyers."*

## Launch warnings (auto-flagged)

- No blurb → run the blurb generation.
- <7 KDP keywords.
- <2 KDP categories.
- Release date < preOrderLead days away.

## The marketing asset bundle

Generated by the writer persona from `arc_summary.md` + `voice.md`, scaffolded by `publish.py`. Output to `publish/marketing/`:

### `blurb.md` — back-cover / Amazon description copy

**Fiction formula (7 parts):**
1. **Hook** — one explosive sentence.
2. **Character** — who the protagonist is.
3. **Setup** — the world/situation.
4. **Inciting Incident** — what changes.
5. **Stakes** — personal AND universal (must be both).
6. **Cliffhanger** — a question that DEMANDS the reader buy the book.
7. **Social proof** — comp titles, blurbs, "for fans of."

Lengths: Amazon description 150–200 words (HTML formatted), back cover 100–150, one-line pitch <25 words, elevator pitch <50 words.

**Nonfiction formula (6 parts):** Problem → Promise → Credibility → Preview (3–5 takeaways) → Transformation → CTA.

**Anti-patterns:** starting with a question, "In a world where…", giving away the ending, using "must" more than once, being vague.

### `ad-copy.md` — Amazon AMS + Meta + BookBub ads

**Platform specs:**
- **Amazon AMS**: headline 150 chars, custom text 150 chars.
- **BookBub Featured Deal**: 500 chars.
- **Facebook/Meta**: primary text 125 chars above fold (up to 500), headline 40 chars, description 30 chars.

**A/B test framework (5 variations):**
- A: benefit-focused
- B: curiosity-focused
- C: social proof
- D: urgency
- E: emotion

Generate 3 short (1-line hook + link) + 3 long (hook → stakes → CTA) variants per launch.

### `social-posts.md` — cover reveal, behind-the-book, excerpt drops

Platform choice by genre:
- **BookTok (TikTok)** — YA, romance, fantasy.
- **Instagram** — literary, cozy, illustrated.
- **X** — SFF, nonfiction.
- **LinkedIn** — business, self-help.

Post types: cover reveal, "behind the book" (from chapter summaries + craft notes), excerpt drops, review resharing.

### `arc-email.md` — ARC team outreach

ARCs (Advance Reader Copies) go out **2–4 weeks pre-launch** to ~20–100 readers via BookFunnel, Prolific Works, or StoryOrigin. Reviews in the first 2 weeks are the **largest lever on Amazon's algorithm**.

Email template elements: the ask (honest review on launch day), the delivery link, the launch date, a reminder 3 days before launch.

## Comp titles — the positioning lever

2–4 recent successful books in your genre, used for:
- Description ("for fans of X and Y").
- Newsletter pitches (BookBub, Bargain Booksy).
- Ad targeting (Amazon "also-boughts" strategy).
- Category/keyword research.

Pick comps published in the **last 2–3 years**, same subgenre, similar audience. Not bestsellers-as-comps (overly broad); not obscure (no signal).

## Author website + mailing list (the long game)

The **single most important long-term owned asset**. Build a simple site (author name or pen name) with:
- Books page
- About page
- Contact
- **Newsletter signup with a reader magnet** (a free short story / novella / checklist in exchange for the email)

Mail via Substack, MailerLite, ConvertKit, or Beehiiv. The list survives any single platform's algorithm change — it's how every subsequent book launches to a warm audience.

## Pricing pulse (the launch-week strategy)

Common pattern: launch at full price ($9.99 ebook), pulse to $4.99 at day 7 to drive volume + algorithm signal, return to full price by day 14. For series openers: $0.99 or perma-free as a permanent funnel into Books 2–N.

## KDP Select / Kindle Unlimited decision

Enrolling the ebook in **KDP Select** (free, 90-day, auto-renewing, **exclusive**) makes it eligible for:
- Kindle Unlimited (paid per-page-read, the KENP royalty pool).
- Kindle Countdown Deals.
- Free Book Promotions.

The tradeoff: you **cannot** sell that ebook on Kobo, Apple Books, Google Play, B&N during the 90-day term. Common for series openers (maximize KU discoverability); less obvious for standalone books where wide distribution matters.

**Royalty rate (35/70%) and Select exclusivity are independent decisions** — common confusion.

## Post-launch cadence

- **+30 days**: AMS campaign review. Kill losers, scale winners.
- **+60 days**: Mid-tail email to engaged readers + social recap.
- **+90 days**: BookBub Featured Deal submission (if eligible — requires minimum review count + rating threshold).
- **Series cadence**: plan the next book's launch to leverage the first book's review base + mailing list. The hardest book to launch is the first; each subsequent one is easier if the reader assets are built.

## What this phase does NOT do

- Does not upload to KDP/Ingram/ACX on your behalf. Produces the files + step-by-step.
- Does not run ads autonomously. Produces the ad copy + targeting recommendations.
- Does not email your list. Produces the email copy.
- Does not generate fake reviews. ARCs are honest reviews from real readers.

The skill builds the kit; the human executes the launch.
