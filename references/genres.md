# Genre defaults

Per-genre voice and structure defaults. Use as starting points during `seed` and `foundation`; the book's actual `voice.md` Part 2 overrides these once discovered. Read this when picking a framework or calibrating the judge.

Each entry: **voice markers, structure, slop watch-outs, judge benchmarks.**

## Fiction

### Fantasy (epic / secondary-world)
- **Voice:** mythic to grounded; register varies. Earned metaphors from the world's materials (iron, salt, ink, glass). Avoid generic-medieval stock.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! spine; Sanderson laws for magic; MICE for nested threads. Min 15 foreshadowing threads.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "tapestry," "realm," "ancient," "powerful," "mysterious," "a sense of magic." Chosen-one/dark-lord opening is a reject at seed.
- **Benchmarks:** Sanderson, Le Guin, Jemisin, Kay, Abercrombie. Target pacing 7+; lore_integration weighted high.

### Fantasy (urban / contemporary)
- **Voice:** grounded, contemporary, with the fantastic treated as ordinary by the POV. Voice markers from the protagonist's job/city.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! or 7-Point. Magic system subtle, often hidden from the mundane world.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "hidden world," "ordinary girl," "everything changed."
- **Benchmarks:** Gaiman, Beagle, Clarke (Susanna).

### Thriller
- **Voice:** spare, present-tense-feeling even in past tense. Short sentences in action; longer in reflection. Every chapter ends on a turn.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! tight; clue ledger (planted clues ≥ used clues). Midpoint reversal is load-bearing.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "heart pounded," "eyes widened," "sweat beaded," "time seemed to slow." Adrenaline clichés.
- **Benchmarks:** Child, Connell, Flynn, Slaughter. Target pacing 7.5+, engagement weighted high.

### Mystery
- **Voice:** observant, often wry. Detective's voice markers from their background.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! + clue ledger + a separate "fair-play" check (reader had the clues to solve it). MICE for nested sub-mysteries.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "the butler did it"-grade obviousness; withholding from the reader unfairly; "a glint in the eye."
- **Benchmarks:** Christie, James, French, Penny.

### Literary fiction
- **Voice:** the point. Distinctive, often first-person or close third. Heavier rhythm tracking; lighter beat scaffolding.
- **Structure:** Story Circle or 7-Point; character arc > plot. Foreshadowing is thematic more than mechanical.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "the weight of," "something shifted," purple prose, "lyrical" as a mask for vague.
- **Benchmarks:** Whatever the user names; default to the modern literary canon. Target prose_quality 8+, voice_adherence 9+.

### Romance
- **Voice:** warm, interior-heavy; dual-POV common. Emotional beats explicit on the page.
- **Structure:** Romance beats are fixed: Meet Cute → Conflict → Midpoint Kiss/Almost → Black Moment → HEA/HFN. Don't skip the Black Moment.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "electric touch," "molten gaze," "heart skipped," "couldn't help but feel."
- **Benchmarks:** the genre's benchmarks shift fast; ask the user. Target character_voice 8+, engagement 8+.

### Horror
- **Voice:** dread-forward. Sensory detail turned to 11. Withhold more than you show until the reveal.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! or 7-Point; the "whiff of death" beat is literal.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "a sense of dread," "the air grew cold," "something in the shadows," gore-as-substitute-for-dread.
- **Benchmarks:** King, Jackson, Machado, Jones (Stephen Graham).

### Science fiction (hard / soft)
- **Voice:** varies. Hard SF: precise, procedural, expert-POV. Soft SF: sociological, idea-driven.
- **Structure:** Save the Cat! or Story Circle. Hard SF: the science must be consistent (treat like a magic system — Sanderson laws apply).
- **Slop watch-outs:** "the vastness of space," "advanced technology," "a beacon of hope," info-dumps disguised as dialogue.
- **Benchmarks:** Le Guin, Liu, Robinson, Chambers, Weir.

## Nonfiction

### Narrative nonfiction
- **Voice:** reportorial to literary. Scene-level storytelling with real people.
- **Structure:** Argument arc, but with extended Scene chapters for key moments.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "against all odds," "it was a quiet morning that would change everything," fabricated quotes (hard failure).
- **Benchmarks:** the user's field. Target clarity 8+, engagement 8+.

### How-to / instructional
- **Voice:** authoritative, second-person, direct.
- **Structure:** Argument arc with Concept → Method → Example → Exercise in each Application chapter.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "in today's fast-paced world," "unlock your potential," "transform your life," vague steps ("be mindful").
- **Benchmarks:** the field's standard references. Target clarity 9+, audience_fit 9+.

### Prescriptive self-help
- **Voice:** coach to confessional. Author credibility on the page (real credentials, real examples).
- **Structure:** Argument arc; methods chapter early; Application chapters with exercises; honesty-about-limitations throughout.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "transform," "unlock," "journey," "powerful," "ancient wisdom," overclaiming results, method-as-panacea.
- **Benchmarks:** the specific sub-genre's standards. Sensitivity/claimer-of-authority check mandatory.

### Memoir
- **Voice:** first-person, distinctive. Voice markers from the author's actual life — discovered in the voice sub-loop.
- **Structure:** Story Circle or Save the Cat! applied to real life. Not autobiography (not chronological cradle-to-now); a shaped story with a thesis.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "that was the moment everything changed," "I would never be the same," hindsight-smoothing of real complexity.
- **Benchmarks:** the user's comparables. Target voice_adherence 9+ (it's the author's voice).

### Prescriptive nonfiction (business / science / history)
- **Voice:** authoritative but not stiff. Expert speaking to a general or professional audience.
- **Structure:** Argument arc; Framework chapter is the spine; Evidence chapters are case studies or data deep-dives.
- **Slop watch-outs:** "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm shift," "in today's competitive landscape," overclaiming from a single study.
- **Benchmarks:** the field's crossover successes. Target claim_support 9+.

## Cross-genre notes

- **Hybrid genres** (romantasy, lit-thriller, narrative-self-help): pick the **primary** genre for structure and the secondary for voice flavor. State the hybrid rule in `outline.md` line 1.
- **Series:** if the book is part of a series, the foreshadowing ledger carries open threads into the next book — flag them `open-series` rather than `open`. The dual-persona review for a series book checks that the *book's own* arc resolves even if series threads stay open.
- **Audience explicitness:** children's (8–12), YA (12–18), adult, new adult. Each has voice and content conventions; name the audience in `research-bible.md` / the seed.
